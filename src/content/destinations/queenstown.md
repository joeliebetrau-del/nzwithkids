## Why Queenstown Works for Families

Most people assume Queenstown is for the bungee-jumping, heli-skiing crowd. It is - but that same energy makes it excellent for kids. The gondola, the luge, the jet boats, the lake, Arrowtown a short drive away - there is more here than you can fit in two days.

## Top Picks for Kids

### Skyline Gondola and Luge
Take the gondola up, ride the luge down, repeat until everyone is hungry. Kids (and adults) will want to do this more than once. Budget for multiple luge rides.

### Shotover Jet
Kids must be 3 years or older. Even young kids who are nervous about the speed usually come off the boat grinning. One of the genuinely great 30-minute experiences in New Zealand.

### Arrowtown
A 20-minute drive from Queenstown, Arrowtown is a beautifully preserved gold rush village with a river you can actually pan for gold in. Slower pace than Queenstown, excellent for an afternoon.

### The Lake
The gravel beach along the waterfront in central Queenstown is underrated. Kids can throw rocks, paddle, and decompress. It's free and endlessly entertaining.

## Logistics

- **Base:** Central Queenstown puts you walking distance from most things.
- **Drives from here:** Milford Sound (4 hrs), Wanaka (1 hr), Te Anau (2 hrs).
- **Book ahead:** Shotover Jet and the luge get busy in peak season.

## Skip

The bungee jumps are not for kids under 13 (and weight minimums apply). The high-end restaurants are great but pricey; Queenstown has good casual options that work better for families.
