## Why Wanaka

Wanaka sits on the southern shore of Lake Wanaka in the Otago region, about an hour from Queenstown by road. It has the same mountain backdrop and lake views as its famous neighbor, about a third of the crowds, and a pace that is genuinely different. After four days of jet boats and luge runs and Milford Sound, four nights in Wanaka felt like exactly the right speed.

We based ourselves at Diamond on Tenby, a short walk from the town center and lake. On quiet mornings, the lake was still enough to reflect the mountains. The kids would have stayed another week.

## Rob Roy Glacier Track

**4 hours return | Mt Aspiring National Park | All ages**

This is the best thing we did in Wanaka and one of the best half-days of the entire trip.

The track heads into Mt Aspiring National Park through silver beech forest before opening into a dramatic hanging valley with views of the Rob Roy Glacier above. The glacier is receding but still extraordinary - ice cliffs and waterfalls tumbling off the moraine directly in front of you.

The older two pushed the pace on the way up. Our eight-year-old made it to the top without complaint and declared it "actually really cool." That is high praise from an eight-year-old who had just spent four days doing luge runs and jet boats.

Practical notes:
- The road to the trailhead is gravel and requires crossing a ford. Standard rental cars manage fine in normal conditions.
- Start early to beat any day-trippers.
- Bring a packed lunch. There are no facilities on the track.
- Layers. Even on a warm day, the valley is cool.

## Puzzle World

**Allow 2-3 hours | All ages**

Puzzle World is the kind of place that looks like a tourist trap from the outside and turns out to be genuinely great. Four interconnected puzzle rooms, an outdoor maze, and a series of optical illusions that actually work. Our twelve-year-old was skeptical and then absorbed for two hours. The ten-year-old attempted the maze multiple times. Our eight-year-old wanted to start over from the beginning.

It is not cheap, but it is worth the price for a rest day when you want something low-key that keeps all three kids engaged.

## Cinema Paradiso

**Evenings | Wanaka town center**

Not a multiplex. A converted house with couches, armchairs, and a small cafe that serves food and freshly baked cookies during the interval. The films feel different when you watch them like this. We took a rest day and ended it here. It was perfect.

## Hook Salmon Fishing Dinner

A uniquely Wanaka experience: a salmon and trout farm where you pay to fish, and they cook whatever you catch. The kids' competitive instincts kicked in immediately. Everyone caught something. Everyone ate well. It was one of those low-key evenings that lands higher on the trip highlight list than the expensive activities.

## The Lake

Free. Infinite. Take the afternoon. The Wanaka waterfront has a beach where you can swim (in summer), skip stones, and watch the mountains. The famous lone tree growing from the lake is a short walk along the shore. The kids spent an hour throwing rocks. Sometimes that is the whole point.

## Getting There

From Mt Cook: 3-hour drive via the Haast Pass road. Build in stops. The pass itself is spectacular.
From Queenstown: 1 hour. Easy day trip access in either direction.

## Practical Notes

- **Wanaka vs. Queenstown:** We deliberately spent more nights in Wanaka. It has the same mountains and lake without the noise. Right choice for a family.
- **Petrol:** Fill up in Wanaka before heading into Mt Aspiring NP.
- **Weather:** The Rob Roy track is manageable in light rain but miserable in heavy weather. Check the forecast and be flexible.
